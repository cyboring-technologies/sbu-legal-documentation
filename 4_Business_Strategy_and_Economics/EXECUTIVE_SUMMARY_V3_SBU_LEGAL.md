## 1. SBU Thesis (≤ 5 lines)

* **Problem:** Legal execution is artificially slow, high-friction, and relationship-dependent. SaaS compounds friction.
* **For whom:** Users facing strict legal deadlines requiring immediate, single-use procedural document generation.
* **Why it exists:** To financially validate the Sovereign Engine architecture, One-Shot Model, and Marketing Parametric Model limits.
* **Defensibility:** Total session incineration (absolute privacy, no retention risk) and immediate low-friction atomic execution.

### Structural Resilience Characteristic

SBU-Legal operates under a structural scale-to-zero architecture. When transactions cease, operational costs collapse to near-zero levels (no payroll dependency, no persistent infrastructure overhead, negligible compute cost).

The marketing model is likewise survival-oriented: organic SEO inventory creation requires time but not structural capital burn, and paid acquisition is explicitly governed by hard stop-loss constraints.

This configuration produces a low fixed-cost base, high gross margin (>90%), and strictly bounded downside exposure. As a result, the probability of structural insolvency is materially lower than SBUs dependent on recurring overhead, retention mechanics, or mandatory acquisition paid to sustain baseline revenue.
