# SBU-Legal — Parametric Marketing Model

**Context:** *This document defines the parametric marketing model and mathematical assumptions for SBU-Legal validation without establishing hardcoded limits, serving as an exploratory financial framework.*

---

## 1. Strategic Invariants

*This layer contains only non-negotiable principles. It overrides emotional hypotheses but does not establish rigid mathematical goals.*

- **Inventory-first SEO dominance:** The model prioritizes indexable surface area first, focusing on technical long-tail keywords with transactional intent, before relying on authority or paid channels.
- **Ads as a probabilistic accelerator:** Early exact match ads function strictly as early validators and probabilistic accelerators to secure real data, not as the dominant dependency.
- **Mandatory stop-loss:** Paid acquisition and marketing experiments must operate under defined stop-losses to control financial exposure without threatening business survival.
- **Operational horizon: March 2026 (Month 0) to December 2026 (Month 9).** If metrics refute the hypotheses, the model must be updated or abandoned.
- **Dominant B2B structural approach (without making it permanent):** B2B acts as the default dominant structural hypothesis for initial resource allocation, but no segment (B2B or B2C) is structurally forced or blocked.
- **High structural margin on the search engine:** The core proposition relies on high margins generated by SEO when ticket sizes are optimized, providing structural oxygen to later buy traffic.

*These rules do not contradict BUSINESS_AXIOMS, UX contracts, or architectural rules.*

# Marketing Control Variables

*These variables represent the levers the Founder can adjust to alter the business projection. They define the operational execution without locking the strategy into a single truth.*

### SEO Production
*   **PAGES_INITIAL**: Starting number of highly specific transactional pages at launch.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Defines the initial indexable surface area and baseline organic traffic potential.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 20 – 50 pages.
*   **PAGES_PER_MONTH**: Pace of sustained content production.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Drives compounding organic traffic growth and dictates founder effort/latency.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 5 – 20 pages/month.
*   **TRAFFIC_PER_PAGE_BASE**: Base estimate of monthly organic traffic per indexed page under normal conditions.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Determines the potential volume of organic traffic.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 10 – 100 visits/month per page.
*   **INDEXATION_FACTOR**: Multiplier factor (0–1+) that represents competition, saturation, algorithm effect, and SEO maturity.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Adjusts actual traffic vs. theoretical traffic.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0.2x – 1.2x.
*   **BACKLINKS_PER_MONTH**: Disciplined acquisition of high-quality, contextual paid backlinks.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Acts as a compounding marginal accelerator. Must not involve exponential scaling to prevent budget absorption. Presents significantly lower financial risk than Google Ads.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0 – 1 backlink/month.
*   **JURISDICTIONS_ACTIVE**: Number of geographic markets explicitly targeted.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Multiplies total addressable search volume but linearly increases required page inventory.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 1 – 3 jurisdictions.
*   **SERVICES_ACTIVE**: Number of specific procedural acts offered.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Expands target query scope; closely tied to initial setup complexity.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 5 – 15 services.

### SEO Operation Costs
*   **SEO_MONTHLY_COST**: Monthly operating cost of SEO production.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Affects baseline marketing burn rate independent of traffic.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $0 – $100.
*   **FOUNDER_TIME_COST**: Value attributed to the founder's time dedicated to SEO.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Establishes opportunity cost and true economic cost of 'free' organic traffic.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $0 – $100.
*   **BACKLINK_COST**: Unit cost per backlink acquired.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Directly impacts the marginal cost of authority acceleration.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $0 – $200.

### Segmentation
*   **SEGMENT_WEIGHT_B2B**: Proportion of effort/capital directed toward the B2B market.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Affects target ticket sizes, assumed conversion rates, and dispute risks.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0% – 100%.
*   **SEGMENT_WEIGHT_B2C**: Proportion of effort/capital directed toward the B2C market.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Impacts the volume of lower-friction, but potentially lower-ticket, transactions.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0% – 100%.
*   **B2B_TICKET_MULTIPLIER**: Relative adjustment of the average ticket size in the B2B segment.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Scales B2B revenue and dictates tolerance for B2B acquisition costs.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 1.0x – 1.5x.
*   **B2C_TICKET_MULTIPLIER**: Relative adjustment of the average ticket size in the B2C segment.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Adjusts B2C revenue to account for price sensitivity in civil law.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0.6x – 1.0x.
*   **B2B_CR_ADJUSTMENT**: Relative adjustment of B2B conversion based on BLENDED_CR.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Accounts for friction and consideration times in corporate transactions.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0.8x – 1.2x.
*   **B2C_CR_ADJUSTMENT**: Relative adjustment of B2C conversion based on BLENDED_CR.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Accounts for higher emotional impulse but potentially higher bounds of friction in civil transactions.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0.8x – 1.3x.

### Pricing
*   **AVG_TICKET**: The modeled base ticket price across segments.
    *   Operational assumption for Marketing Plan v3: AVG_TICKET = $25.
    *   *Financial Impact:* The most sensitive lever for structural margin and MRR validity. Directly governs CAC viability.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $15.00 – $60.00 USD.
*   **PRICE_TEST_ACTIVE**: Boolean or multiplier representing active A/B testing on pricing.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Temporarily affects conversion rates to discover optimal price elasticity.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 1.0x – 1.5x variation.
*   **EXECUTION_VARIABLE_COST**: The unit cost of delivering the legal service per transaction.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Sets the absolute floor for gross margin before marketing and operating expenses.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $0.50 – $2.00.

### Paid Acquisition
*   **ADS_MONTHLY_BUDGET**: Maximum capital deployed for probabilistic validation.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Cap on financial burn rate during the latency period.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $0 – $500/month.
*   **CPC_ESTIMATED**: Assumed cost per click for exact match terms.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Determines the volume of traffic acquired per budget unit and directly establishes CAC.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $0.50 – $3.00.
*   **STOP_LOSS**: Hard limit on cumulative ad spend without achieving a target CAC.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Protects the treasury by permanently halting unprofitable probabilistic acceleration.
    *   *Suggested Range:* $300 – $1,000 cumulative.

### CRO
*   **BASE_CR**: Assumed organic conversion rate before interventions.
    *   *Financial Impact:* The foundational baseline for all revenue.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 1% – 3%.
*   **POST_CRO_CR**: Target conversion rate after deep semantic structuring, friction reduction, and trust building.
    *   *Financial Impact:* The primary multiplier for existing traffic. A marginal increase radically transforms MRR without added acquisition cost.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 3% – 7%.
*   **CRO_START_MONTH**: The timeline for executing primary structural CRO interventions.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Determines when the compounding effects of increased CR begin to affect revenue.
    *   *Suggested Range:* Month 2 – Month 4.

### Geographic Expansion
*   **CITY_EXPANSION_MONTH**: The trigger month for duplicating the model to a new jurisdiction.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Governs the timeline for CAPEX (page cloning) vs. OPEX (revenue from new markets).
    *   *Suggested Range:* Month 4 – Month 6.
*   **NEW_CITY_TRAFFIC_MULTIPLIER**: Expected percentage of the base city's traffic achieved in the new jurisdiction.
    *   *Financial Impact:* Projects the ROI of geographic scaling.
    *   *Suggested Range:* 0.5x – 1.0x.

# Canonical Financial Model

*This parametric model calculates projected outcomes cleanly using exclusively the variables defined above. Formulas depend exclusively on Layer 2 variables without fixed numbers.*

*   **TOTAL_PAGES_MONTH_n** = (`PAGES_INITIAL` + (`PAGES_PER_MONTH` * n)) * `JURISDICTIONS_ACTIVE`
*   **ORGANIC_TRAFFIC_MONTH_n** = `TOTAL_PAGES_MONTH_n` * `TRAFFIC_PER_PAGE_BASE` * `INDEXATION_FACTOR`
*   **PAID_TRAFFIC** = `ADS_MONTHLY_BUDGET` / `CPC_ESTIMATED` (If cumulative spend < `STOP_LOSS`)
*   **TOTAL_TRAFFIC** = `ORGANIC_TRAFFIC_MONTH_n` + `PAID_TRAFFIC`
*   **BLENDED_CR** = Base calculation on `BASE_CR`, switching to `POST_CRO_CR` after `CRO_START_MONTH`.
*   **B2B_SALES** = `TOTAL_TRAFFIC` * `SEGMENT_WEIGHT_B2B` * `BLENDED_CR` * `B2B_CR_ADJUSTMENT`
*   **B2C_SALES** = `TOTAL_TRAFFIC` * `SEGMENT_WEIGHT_B2C` * `BLENDED_CR` * `B2C_CR_ADJUSTMENT`
*   **SALES** = `B2B_SALES` + `B2C_SALES`
*   **B2B_REVENUE** = `B2B_SALES` * `AVG_TICKET` * `B2B_TICKET_MULTIPLIER`
*   **B2C_REVENUE** = `B2C_SALES` * `AVG_TICKET` * `B2C_TICKET_MULTIPLIER`
*   **REVENUE** = `B2B_REVENUE` + `B2C_REVENUE`
*   **VARIABLE_COST** = `SALES` * `EXECUTION_VARIABLE_COST`
*   **MARKETING_COST** = `ADS_MONTHLY_BUDGET` + `SEO_MONTHLY_COST` + `FOUNDER_TIME_COST` + (`BACKLINKS_PER_MONTH` * `BACKLINK_COST`)
*   **NET_CASH** = `REVENUE` - `VARIABLE_COST` - `MARKETING_COST`
*   **MRR** = The stabilized recurring monthly variation of `REVENUE` across all channels.

# Scenario Simulation Framework

*Changing the variables in Layer 2 produces a different projection. The model accommodates adapting assumptions dynamically without having to change the document structure.*

### Conservative Scenario
*   **Variables Focus:** Maximize safety, restrict capital deployment.
*   **Adjustments:** `PAGES_INITIAL` at lower range, `PAGES_PER_MONTH` lowered, `ADS_MONTHLY_BUDGET` minimized, `BASE_CR` maintained exclusively.
*   **Description:** Slow SEO indexation, reliance entirely on Base CR, ads are turned off quickly due to hitting the `STOP_LOSS`. Validates minimum survival state. Growth is slow, but cash burn is strictly contained.

### Baseline Scenario
*   **Variables Focus:** Steady, median execution with gradual adjustments.
*   **Adjustments:** Median values across all levers. `CRO_START_MONTH` activates normally. B2B and B2C segments balance organically according to testing metrics.
*   **Description:** Represents the highest-probability operational trajectory. Steady inventory growth and median CR combine to steadily increase revenue. Operational horizon: March 2026 (Month 0) to December 2026 (Month 9).

### Aggressive Scenario
*   **Variables Focus:** Maximize validated growth via reinvestment and expanded scope.
*   **Adjustments:** `PAGES_INITIAL` maxed out, `POST_CRO_CR` aggressively targeted, `ADS_MONTHLY_BUDGET` scales continuously due to validated healthy CPA.
*   **Description:** High CR unlocks early Ads scaling. Geographic expansion (`CITY_EXPANSION_MONTH`) happens quickly due to robust early metrics. Simulates rapid market capture based on immediate initial success.

# Sensitivity Drivers

*This framework explains how the manipulation of specific variables accelerates, decelerates, or structurally alters the financial viability of the model.*

### 1. CR Sensitivity
Marginal variations in conversion exponentially impact revenue due to the compounding effect on high-margin traffic.
*   **BASE_CR & POST_CRO_CR:** Moving along the target range directly multiplies MRR without increasing acquisition costs or inventory burn rate.
*   **B2B_CR_ADJUSTMENT & B2C_CR_ADJUSTMENT:** Relative elasticity here determines which segment naturally absorbs the traffic efficiently. A micro-change in these adjustments defines segment dominance logically.

### 2. Traffic Sensitivity
The absolute volume of potential clients entering the funnel.
*   **TRAFFIC_PER_PAGE_BASE:** Drives the theoretical ceiling of structural autonomy.
*   **INDEXATION_FACTOR:** The most profound source of uncertainty and risk in the organic model. Its variation directly expands or collapses the probability of avoiding ad dependency.
*   **PAGES_PER_MONTH:** Controls the speed at which the `TRAFFIC_PER_PAGE_BASE` accumulates compound returns.

### 3. Ticket Sensitivity
Determines the structural elasticity of the business margins and maximum tolerable acquisition costs.
*   **AVG_TICKET:** Directly dictates the breakeven threshold for probabilistic ads. Moving this lever up mathematically expands the oxygen for expensive keywords and isolates the business from traffic drops.
*   **B2B_TICKET_MULTIPLIER & B2C_TICKET_MULTIPLIER:** Controls how much pricing power can be exerted on specific segments, influencing where the most lucrative capital allocation lies.

### 4. CPC Sensitivity
Impact on financial burn and the risk inherent in probabilistic validation.
*   **CPC_ESTIMATED & ADS_MONTHLY_BUDGET:** Together, they govern CAC and the burn rate during the latency phase. Extreme sensitivity here indicates a need to rely more heavily on `ORGANIC_TRAFFIC_MONTH_n` or trigger the `STOP_LOSS`.

### 5. Indexing Latency Sensitivity
The impact on the time inflection point before structural survival is met.
*   **INDEXATION_FACTOR, PAGES_INITIAL & PAGES_PER_MONTH:** These three levers collectively dictate how many months of zero-cash burn are required before organic traffic bridges the $1,000 MRR threshold. Increasing `PAGES_INITIAL` and `PAGES_PER_MONTH` attempts to compress the time-axis, fighting against a sluggish `INDEXATION_FACTOR`.
